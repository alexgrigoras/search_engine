<p><i><b><a href="/wiki/Resident_Evil_5" title="Resident Evil 5">Resident Evil 5</a></b></i> is a <a href="/wiki/Third-person_shooter" title="Third-person shooter">third-person shooter</a> video game developed and published by <a href="/wiki/Capcom" title="Capcom">Capcom</a>, and produced by <a href="/wiki/Jun_Takeuchi" title="Jun Takeuchi">Jun Takeuchi</a> <i>(pictured)</i>. It was released for <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_3" title="PlayStation 3">PlayStation 3</a> and <a href="/wiki/Xbox_360" title="Xbox 360">Xbox 360</a> consoles on March 5, 2009. The plot involves an investigation of a terrorist threat by counter-terrorist agents <a href="/wiki/Chris_Redfield" title="Chris Redfield">Chris Redfield</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sheva_Alomar" title="Sheva Alomar">Sheva Alomar</a> in Kijuju, a fictional region of Africa. It is the seventh major installment in the <i><a href="/wiki/Resident_Evil" title="Resident Evil">Resident Evil</a></i> series, and the first designed for two-player <a href="/wiki/Cooperative_gameplay" title="Cooperative gameplay">cooperative gameplay</a>. Critics described the game as closer to the <a href="/wiki/Action_game" title="Action game">action genre</a> than the <a href="/wiki/Survival_horror" title="Survival horror">survival horror</a> of other games in the series. <i>Resident Evil 5</i> had a mostly positive reception, although it was criticized for problems with its controls. The game was re-released for <a href="/wiki/Nvidia_Shield_(set-top_box)" title="Nvidia Shield (set-top box)">Nvidia Shield</a>, <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_4" title="PlayStation 4">PlayStation 4</a> and <a href="/wiki/Xbox_One" title="Xbox One">Xbox One</a> in 2016. As of September 2018, it had sold over 7.4 million units, making it Capcom's second best-selling game and the best-selling game of the <i>Resident Evil</i> franchise. <p><b><a href="/wiki/March_5" title="March 5">March 5</a></b>: <b><a href="/wiki/Shrove_Tuesday" title="Shrove Tuesday">Shrove Tuesday</a></b> (Western Christianity, 2019); <b><a href="/wiki/Lei_Feng" title="Lei Feng">Learn from Lei Feng Day</a></b> in China; <b><a href="/wiki/St_Piran%27s_Day" title="St Piran&#39;s Day">St Piran's Day</a></b> in <a href="/wiki/Cornwall" title="Cornwall">Cornwall</a>, United Kingdom
</p>
<div style="float:right;margin-left:0.5em;" id="mp-otd-img">
<div class="thumbinner mp-thumb" style="background: transparent; border: none; padding: 0; max-width: 148px;">

</div>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/1279" title="1279">1279</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Livonian_Order" title="Livonian Order">Livonian branch</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Teutonic_Order" title="Teutonic Order">Teutonic Order</a> suffered a great loss when 71 knights died in the <b><a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Aizkraukle" title="Battle of Aizkraukle">Battle of Aizkraukle</a></b>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1770" title="1770">1770</a> – The <b><a href="/wiki/Boston_Massacre" title="Boston Massacre">Boston Massacre</a></b> <i>(engraving shown)</i>: British soldiers fired into a crowd in <a href="/wiki/Boston" title="Boston">Boston</a>, <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Massachusetts_Bay" title="Province of Massachusetts Bay">Massachusetts</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1811" title="1811">1811</a> – <a href="/wiki/Peninsular_War" title="Peninsular War">Peninsular War</a>: In the <b><a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Barrosa" title="Battle of Barrosa">Battle of Barrosa</a></b>, an Anglo-Spanish-Portuguese force trying to lift the <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_C%C3%A1diz" title="Siege of Cádiz">Siege of Cádiz</a> defeated a French attack but could not break the siege itself.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1966" title="1966">1966</a> – <b><a href="/wiki/BOAC_Flight_911" title="BOAC Flight 911">BOAC Flight 911</a></b> disintegrated and crashed near <a href="/wiki/Mount_Fuji" title="Mount Fuji">Mount Fuji</a> shortly after departure from <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_International_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Tokyo International Airport">Tokyo International Airport</a>, killing all 113 passengers and 11 crew members on board.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1975" title="1975">1975</a> – Computer hobbyists in <a href="/wiki/Silicon_Valley" title="Silicon Valley">Silicon Valley</a> held the first meeting of the <b><a href="/wiki/Homebrew_Computer_Club" title="Homebrew Computer Club">Homebrew Computer Club</a></b>, whose members would go on to have great influence on the development of the <a href="/wiki/Personal_computer" title="Personal computer">personal computer</a>.</li></ul>
